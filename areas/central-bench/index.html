Parks in Central Bench

The City of Boise lists 12 parks in Central Bench, about 77 acres in total. 10 have playground equipment, 3 have a restroom that stays open through the winter, and 2 have a splash pad, fountain or pool. Every park below is scored on what matters with kids.
🌳 12 parks 🛝 10 playgrounds 🚻 3 year-round restrooms 💦 2 with water play

Best with kids in Central Bench

The highest Parent Scores in this part of town — the score weighs playground equipment, tree cover, open grass and restrooms.

  1. 1 Terry Day Park 9.3/10 A Federal Way favorite: bonded-rubber playground, a fishing pond, picnic shelter, tennis courts, two parking lots and a heated year-round restroom.
  2. 2 Cassia Park 9/10 The 2020 playground includes a wheelchair-accessible We-Go-Round spinner on bonded rubber surfacing — one of the Bench's best accessible playgrounds, with a walking path and shady picnic spots.
  3. 3 Franklin Park 8.8/10 Central Bench standout: bonded-rubber 2022 playground, misters, a quarter-mile paved loop, outdoor gym, bocce courts and a year-round restroom.
  4. 4 Phillippi Park 8.8/10 One of the Bench's best accessible playgrounds — universally accessible design with a Ten Spin on bonded rubber (2020) — plus a reservable shelter, courts and an hourly dog area.
  5. 5 Owyhee Park 8.5/10 Bench park near Vista with playground equipment for both little kids and big kids, four tennis courts and practice fields.

Common questions

How many parks are there in Central Bench?

The City of Boise lists 12 parks in Central Bench, covering about 77 acres. 10 of them have playground equipment.

Which park in Central Bench is best with young kids?

Terry Day Park has the highest Parent Score in Central Bench at 9.3 out of 10, based on its playground, tree cover, open grass and restrooms. The full ranking for the area is above.

Which parks in Central Bench have restrooms open in winter?

3 of the 12 parks in Central Bench have heated restrooms that stay open year-round: Terry Day Park, Franklin Park, Morris Hill Park.

Are there splash pads in Central Bench?

2 parks in Central Bench have water play: Franklin Park, Borah Park. Water features run Memorial Day through Labor Day.

Which parks in Central Bench have the most shade?

9 parks are rated leafy in Central Bench, meaning dense mature tree cover measured from the city tree inventory: Terry Day Park, Cassia Park, Phillippi Park, Owyhee Park, Shoshone Park, Bowden Park, Morris Hill Park, Platt Gardens and others.
