Ann Morrison Park

1000 N Americana Blvd, Boise, ID 83706 · 148.1 acres · North River

Parent notes

Boise's big classic park. The interactive fountain (Memorial Day–Labor Day, sunrise to sunset) is a summer ritual — bring towels and a change of clothes. The playground has bonded rubber surfacing, and the huge lawns absorb any amount of energy.

Common questions about Ann Morrison Park

Does Ann Morrison Park have a restroom?

Yes. Ann Morrison Park has a heated restroom building that stays open year-round, including through the winter.

Does Ann Morrison Park have a playground?

Yes. Ann Morrison Park has equipment for both toddlers (ages 2–5) and big kids (ages 5–12), including slides, swings. The surfacing is bonded rubber, which is stroller and wheelchair friendly. The newest structure was installed in 2002.

Does Ann Morrison Park have a splash pad?

Ann Morrison Park has a interactive fountain. It runs sunrise–sunset, memorial day–labor day and is free to use.

Is there shade at Ann Morrison Park?

Ann Morrison Park has some shade — a mix of trees and open lawn. The city tree inventory lists 1,208 trees in the park.

Where do you park at Ann Morrison Park?

Large lots at the Americana Blvd and Royal Blvd entrances.

How big is Ann Morrison Park?

Ann Morrison Park is 148.1 acres. It is classified by the city as a large urban park and is located in the North River area of Boise at 1000 N Americana Blvd.
