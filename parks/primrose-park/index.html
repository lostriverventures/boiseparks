Primrose Park

6020 N Gary Ln, Boise, ID 83714 · 1.5 acres · North River

Parent notes

Small but thoughtfully rebuilt in 2024 — the playground has accessible features, and there's an outdoor gym, a pollinator garden, a shelter and van-accessible ADA parking.

Common questions about Primrose Park

Does Primrose Park have a restroom?

Primrose Park has a restroom that is open in the warm months. The building is winterized for the cold months, but the city leaves a portable toilet on site.

Does Primrose Park have a playground?

Yes. Primrose Park has equipment for big kids (ages 5–12), including slides, swings. The surfacing is bonded rubber, which is stroller and wheelchair friendly. The newest structure was installed in 2024.

Is there shade at Primrose Park?

Primrose Park has some shade — a mix of trees and open lawn. The city tree inventory lists 24 trees in the park.

Where do you park at Primrose Park?

Small lot with ADA van-accessible parking (per the city).

How big is Primrose Park?

Primrose Park is 1.5 acres. It is classified by the city as a neighborhood park and is located in the North River area of Boise at 6020 N Gary Ln.

For the city's official write-up and any current alerts, see the Primrose Park page at Boise Parks and Recreation.
