1

Terry Day Park

Central Bench · 1225 S Federal Way

9.3/10

A Federal Way favorite: bonded-rubber playground, a fishing pond, picnic shelter, tennis courts, two parking lots and a heated year-round restroom.

  • · Built for big kids (5–12)
  • · Merry-go-round / spinner, net climber, slides, swings, track ride / zipline
  • · Bonded rubber surfacing — stroller and wheelchair friendly
  • · Newest structure installed 2015
  • · Leafy — lots of mature tree cover
  • · Heated restrooms, open year-round
2

Camel's Back Park

North River · 1200 W Heron St

9/10

North End favorite: a playground with bonded rubber surfacing under mature trees, tennis courts, and the famous hill — kids climb it in summer and sled it after a good snow. Hyde Park's treats are a block away. Heated restrooms stay open all year.

  • · Equipment for toddlers and big kids
  • · Slides, swings, track ride / zipline
  • · Bonded rubber surfacing — stroller and wheelchair friendly
  • · Newest structure installed 2000
  • · Leafy — lots of mature tree cover
  • · Heated restrooms, open year-round
3

Cassia Park

Central Bench · 4600 W Camas St

9/10

The 2020 playground includes a wheelchair-accessible We-Go-Round spinner on bonded rubber surfacing — one of the Bench's best accessible playgrounds, with a walking path and shady picnic spots.

  • · Equipment for toddlers and big kids
  • · Merry-go-round / spinner, slides, swings
  • · Bonded rubber surfacing — stroller and wheelchair friendly
  • · Newest structure installed 2020
  • · Leafy — lots of mature tree cover
  • · Seasonal restrooms; portable toilet in winter

Common questions

What is the highest-rated playground in Boise?

Terry Day Park in Central Bench, at 9.3 out of 10. It combines strong playground equipment with tree cover, open grass and a year-round restroom. The top 12 are listed above with the details for each.

How are these playgrounds ranked?

Each park gets a 0–10 Parent Score computed from official city data: the playground (up to 3.5 points, based on age range, accessible surfacing and install year), tree cover (2.5), open grass (1.5) and restrooms (1.5). Water features, shelters and trails are bonuses capped at +1, so a park is not marked down for lacking them. Parking is listed but not scored.

Which Boise playgrounds have equipment for toddlers?

The shortlist above covers parks with equipment for ages 2–5 that also have shade and a restroom. Each park page states whether the equipment is built for toddlers, ages 5–12, or both.

Are any Boise playgrounds fenced?

The city's open data does not record playground fencing, so this site does not list it. Park size and layout are on each park page.

Which Boise playgrounds have shade?

Shade ratings are calculated from the city tree inventory as trunk diameter per acre. Parks rated leafy have dense mature canopy; those are listed in the shade shortlist above.
